A bit on contexts: "scope context" vs "flexibility context".
	Smallest contexts (by span) to largest are: scope contexts, flexibility contexts, iteration contexts, exploration context.
	Every new flexibility context is also a scope context.
	x:int => {a:int, a=x} # Here, everything in 'a:int, a=3' is in a new flexibility context, but the 'x:int' is outside of it.
        x:int; let(y:=3) do x=y; # Here, everything is in the same flexibility context, but 'let(y:=3) do x=y' is a new scope context.
	let(x:=3) do 4; x # Error: the right-hand x isn't visible in this scope.
